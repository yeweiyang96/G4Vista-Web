Research Report

Growth temperature-G4 density signals by taxonomy rank

Abstract

This exploratory screen examined BacDive-derived growth-temperature analyses to identify taxonomy subsets where microbial G4 density metrics show a strong positive association with growth temperature. The analysis does not establish causality; it highlights candidate lineages for biological interpretation.

Main finding

Peptococcaceae, Photobacterium, the Aquificota/Aquificia/Aquificales lineage, and several environmental Gammaproteobacteria groups showed repeated positive Spearman correlations across multiple G4 density outcomes.

Methods

Taxonomy-rank screening criteria

Correlation rows retained

  • arrow_upward Positive growth-temperature/G4 associations only.
  • functions Spearman rho of at least 0.5 after requiring at least 20 genomes.
  • verified FDR-adjusted q-value of 0.05 or lower.

Density outcomes retained

  • density_medium Overall, gene, upstream, downstream, and intergenic G4 density per Mb.
  • filter_alt_off Raw G4 count was excluded because genome size can dominate that metric.
  • rule Taxa were retained when at least 3 density outcomes passed the screen.

Discussion

Taxonomic interpretation

local_fire_department

Thermophilic lineages

Aquificae class, Aquificales order, and Aquificae phylum are best interpreted as the same Aquificota/Aquificia high-temperature bacterial lineage observed at multiple ranks.

waves

Marine and environmental groups

Photobacterium and Vibrionales point to related marine Gammaproteobacteria, while Cellvibrionales, Rhodanobacteraceae, and Lysobacterales represent environmental lineages.

biotech

Archaeal candidate

Methanobacteriaceae should be reported separately from bacterial groups because it is a methanogenic archaeal family.

Conclusion

Interpretation notes

  • The screen supports a correlation-based statement: several taxonomy subsets show consistent positive Spearman correlations between growth temperature and G4 density.
  • The result should not be phrased as evidence that temperature directly increases G4 formation.
  • Parent-child taxonomy signals, such as Photobacterium within Vibrionales or Rhodanobacteraceae within Lysobacterales, may not be independent discoveries.
